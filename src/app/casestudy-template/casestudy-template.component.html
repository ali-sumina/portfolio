Memory Problem

Stereotypes

It is essential to address a prevalent notion often expressed by some mature individuals: the belief that they are not wired for learning.

Science

Memory processes in mature individuals can show declines in certain abilities, such as episodic memory, working memory challenges, slower information processing, and increased susceptibility to interference.

But what if...

  • ...mature individuals have the potential to excel in learning new information?

  • ...I challenge stereotypes by understanding how memory functions in mature individuals?

  • ...the perceived problem stems from subjective experiences rather than factual evidence?

Breaking Stereotypes

I decided to investigate how adults' memory functions by referencing lectures and scientific videos on this topic.

Alan Castel – How we learn as we age

https://youtu.be/g5jUjOr7G8M
Conclusion

Mature people tend to have more selective memory, and if they learn something, they are aware of their goals much better.

Justin Sung – Stop studying. Start learning

https://youtu.be/TQXMl4GycD0
Conclusion

Frustration occurs when we randomly absorb information that has no bonds. Learning works when we create or look for relationships between the information we are trying to chunk. By noticing these bonds, we can strengthen them through thoughtful note-taking.
